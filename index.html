About Us

Matugga Hills Secondary School was founded on 2nd February 2010 under the name Hillstone Matugga Secondary School. The school later changed its name to Matugga Hills Secondary School to clearly reflect its location and identity within the community.
The school was established by five committed founders under the leadership of Mr. Arinaitwe Kenneth, who later changed his career to banking but continues to serve as the Managing Director of the school to date.;

View More

The school began operations in rented premises with a few small classrooms. It opened with 100 students, offering classes from Senior One to Senior Six, and was supported by 13 dedicated teachers. Despite operating in modest facilities, the staff remained committed to providing quality education.

The first Headteacher, Mr. Kato Edward, was appointed in 2010. Having already spent three years in the teaching profession before joining the school, he has continued to serve faithfully as the school's Headteacher to date.

Although the school did not initially have its own UNEB examination centre, students consistently achieved excellent academic results while sitting their national examinations at other registered schools. In its second year, the best Uganda Certificate of Education (UCE) candidate scored 17 aggregates, while the best Uganda Advanced Certificate of Education (UACE) candidate obtained 22 points.


Relocation and acquiring center number
In 2017, the school relocated from the rented premises to its current, larger and more conducive campus. At the time of the relocation, student enrolment had reduced to 82 students. Through consistent improvement in academic performance, discipline and school management, enrolment steadily increased to over 600 students, where it stands today.

The school's growth continued with significant milestones. In 2023, Matugga Hills Secondary School was granted its own UCE Examination Centre (U4036). In 2024, it also acquired its own UACE Examination Centre (U4036). Academic excellence has continued to improve over the years. In 2025, the school celebrated one of its best performances when a candidate scored 9As in the UCE examinations, while another obtained 20 points in the UACE examinations.
