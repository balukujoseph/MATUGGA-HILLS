About Matugga Hills Secondary School

Building Excellence Since 2010

Our Story

Matugga Hills Secondary School was founded on 2nd February 2010 under the name Hillstone Matugga Secondary School.

The school later changed its name to Matugga Hills Secondary School to clearly reflect its location and identity within the community.

The school was established by five committed founders under the leadership of Mr. Arinaitwe Kenneth, who later changed his career to banking but continues to serve as the Managing Director of the school to date.

📖 Read Our Full History

Our Humble Beginning

The school began operations in rented premises with a few small classrooms. It opened with 100 students, offering classes from Senior One to Senior Six, and was supported by 13 dedicated teachers.

Despite operating in modest facilities, the staff remained committed to providing quality education.

The first Headteacher, Mr. Kato Edward, was appointed in 2010. Having already spent three years in the teaching profession before joining the school, he has continued to serve faithfully as the school's Headteacher.

Although the school did not initially have its own UNEB Examination Centre, students consistently achieved excellent academic results while sitting their examinations at other registered schools.

In its second year, the best UCE candidate scored 17 aggregates, while the best UACE candidate obtained 22 points.

Growth & Milestones

2017

The school relocated from rented premises to its current, larger and more conducive campus.

At that time enrolment had reduced to 82 students, but through discipline, improved academic performance and good management, enrolment steadily increased to over 600 students.

2023

Granted its own UCE Examination Centre (U4036).

2024

Granted its own UACE Examination Centre (U4036).

2025

One candidate scored 9As in UCE while another obtained 20 points in UACE.
