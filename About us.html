Our Story

Two founders' visions, one enduring name

Matugga Hills Secondary School was founded on 2nd February 2010 under the name Hillstone Matugga Secondary School. The school later changed its name to Matugga Hills Secondary School to clearly reflect its location and identity within the community.

It was established by five committed founders under the leadership of Mr. Arinaitwe Kenneth, who later changed his career to banking but continues to serve as the school's Managing Director with dedication to this day.

The school began operations in rented premises with a few small classrooms — opening with 100 students from Senior One to Senior Six, supported by 13 dedicated teachers.

Despite operating in modest facilities, the staff remained committed to providing quality education. The first Headteacher, Mr. Kato Edward, was appointed in 2010. Having already spent three years in the teaching profession before joining the school, he has continued to serve faithfully as the school's Headteacher.

Although the school did not initially have its own UNEB examination centre, students consistently achieved excellent academic results while sitting their examinations at other registered schools. In its second year, the best UCE candidate scored 17 aggregates, while the best UACE candidate obtained 22 points.

Today, Matugga Hills Secondary School continues to grow as a centre of academic excellence, discipline and character development — remaining committed to nurturing responsible, competent and morally upright citizens.

Growth & Milestones

The school relocated from rented premises to its current, larger and more conducive campus. Enrolment had reduced to 82 students at the time, but through discipline, improved academic performance and good management, it steadily increased to over 600 students.
